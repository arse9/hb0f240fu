Physics Formula Sheet

Ekin: 1⁄2mv²

Formula: Ekin = 1⁄2mv²

Explanation: Kinetic energy is the energy an object possesses due to its motion. It is directly proportional to the mass and the square of the object's velocity.

Units:

  • m = mass in kg
  • v = velocity in m/s

Example: For a mass of 5 kg moving at a velocity of 10 m/s:

Calculation: Ekin = ½ × 5 × 10² = ½ × 5 × 100 = 250 J

Epot: mgh

Formula: Epot = mgh

Explanation: Potential energy is the energy stored in an object due to its position or configuration. The potential energy is directly proportional to the mass, gravitational force, and height.

Units:

  • m = mass in kg
  • g = gravitational acceleration in m/s² (9.82 m/s² in Denmark)
  • h = height in meters

Example: For a mass of 3 kg at a height of 10 m:

Calculation: Epot = 3 × 9.82 × 10 = 294.6 J

Emech: Ekin + Epot

Formula: Emech = Ekin + Epot

Explanation: The mechanical energy of a system is the sum of its kinetic and potential energy. It is a measure of the total energy in a system.

Units:

  • Emech = Joules (J)

Example: If Ekin = 250 J and Epot = 294.6 J:

Calculation: Emech = 250 + 294.6 = 544.6 J

Etherm: mcΔT

Formula: Etherm = mcΔT

Explanation: The thermal energy change is the product of mass, specific heat capacity, and temperature change.

Units:

  • m = mass in kg
  • c = specific heat capacity in J/(kg·°C)
  • ΔT = change in temperature in °C

Example: For a mass of 2 kg with a specific heat capacity of 4200 J/(kg·°C) and a temperature change of 5°C:

Calculation: Etherm = 2 × 4200 × 5 = 42000 J

ΔT = T2 - T1

Formula: ΔT = T2 - T1

Explanation: The change in temperature is the difference between the final and initial temperatures.

Units:

  • T2, T1 = temperatures in °C

Example: If T2 = 30°C and T1 = 20°C:

Calculation: ΔT = 30 - 20 = 10°C

Eelec: V×I×Δt

Formula: Eelec = V×I×Δt

Explanation: The electric energy is the product of voltage, current, and time.

Units:

  • V = voltage in volts (V)
  • I = current in amperes (A)
  • Δt = time in seconds (s)

Example: For a voltage of 12 V, current of 3 A, and time of 10 s:

Calculation: Eelec = 12 × 3 × 10 = 360 J

P: ΔE / Δt

Formula: P = ΔE / Δt

Explanation: Power is the rate at which energy is transferred or converted.

Units:

  • ΔE = energy in joules (J)
  • Δt = time in seconds (s)

Example: If ΔE = 500 J and Δt = 10 s:

Calculation: P = 500 / 10 = 50 W

η: Euseful / Einput × 100%

Formula: η = Euseful / Einput × 100%

Explanation: Efficiency is the ratio of useful energy output to total energy input.

Units:

  • Euseful, Einput = energy in joules (J)

Example: If the useful energy is 400 J and the input energy is 500 J:

Calculation: η = (400 / 500) × 100% = 80%

Important: The % cannot exceed 100%!

Unit Conversions

Time Conversion

Hours to Seconds:

To convert 2 hours to seconds, multiply 2 by 3600.

Formula: seconds = hours × 3600

Calculation:

seconds = 2 × 3600 = 7200 seconds

Minutes to Seconds:

To convert 5 minutes to seconds, multiply 5 by 60.

Formula: seconds = minutes × 60

Calculation:

seconds = 5 × 60 = 300 seconds
Distance Conversion

Kilometers to Meters:

To convert 3 kilometers to meters, multiply 3 by 1000.

Formula: meters = kilometers × 1000

Calculation:

meters = 3 × 1000 = 3000 meters

Meters to Centimeters:

To convert 4 meters to centimeters, multiply 4 by 100.

Formula: centimeters = meters × 100

Calculation:

centimeters = 4 × 100 = 400 centimeters
Temperature Conversion

Celsius to Fahrenheit:

To convert a temperature of 25°C to Fahrenheit, multiply 25 by 1.8 and then add 32.

Formula: F = (C × 1.8) + 32

Calculation:

F = (25 × 1.8) + 32 = 45 + 32 = 77°F

Fahrenheit to Celsius:

To convert a temperature of 77°F to Celsius, subtract 32 from 77, and then divide by 1.8.

Formula: C = (F - 32) ÷ 1.8

Calculation:

C = (77 - 32) ÷ 1.8 = 45 ÷ 1.8 = 25°C
Power Conversion

Kilowatts to Watts:

To convert 5 kilowatts (kW) to watts (W), multiply 5 by 1000.

Formula: W = kW × 1000

Calculation:

W = 5 × 1000 = 5000 W

Watts to Kilowatts:

To convert 5000 watts (W) to kilowatts (kW), divide 5000 by 1000.

Formula: kW = W ÷ 1000

Calculation:

kW = 5000 ÷ 1000 = 5 kW
